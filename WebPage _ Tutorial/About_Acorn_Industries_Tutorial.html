Product Vision:

Create tentative schedules that takes into consideration the difficultly of classes so that students can make use of it once they have decided on a major. These schedules take into consideration the difficulty of the classes. The consideration of the difficulty result in a balanced schedule that allow the students to advance in their major while reducing the burden of having to research the classes they want to take.

Product Mission:

Provide a desktop application that can be used by students majoring in any of the three fields offered by the Computer Science and Engineering department at the University of Texas at Arlington. The desktop application helps students reduce the burden of having to research the classes they want to take by creating balanced schedule for each semester.

Success Criteria:

This idea was generated from the need of students to plan their academic schedule in a way that allows them to advance in the major while reducing the difficulty of each semester. New students in particular will benefit from this application since it will provide them with a guide on how to structure their academic work through each semester. Both students that decide to take the classes suggested by the application, and those that have not used the schedule will benefit from its suggestions and hopefully understand how to create their own schedule.

Product Functionalities:

  • The degree planner can be saved to continue at a later date.
  • The system can load a preexisting degree planner file.
  • Generate a sample degree plan for Computer Science Students at UT Arlington.
  • Allow students to select courses for any semester they would like to ease the process for advisor/student appointments.

Tutorial:

1. User may start off by selecting courses that they have previously taken or if they have a previous session they would like to load, it can be done here.

2. A window will pop up with an organized list of courses to select from. The user may find all the courses they have previously taken and confirm once they have completed.

3. The next step would be to create an initial schedule which would then prompt the user to enter the semester and year that they plan to begin their degree planning.

4. Once the user enters their semester and year, for example Spring 2018, a tab will be created and a course selection will be generated.

5. Upon clicking a course, a description will be displayed showing the course information and requirements needed to take the course. A User may select courses and deselect courses by double clicking the course name.

6. Once the courses have been selected for a semester, the user will click "Schedule" and "Add new schedule to create a new semester and year tab in sequential order.

7. If a user decides they are not sure about their schedule, they may click "Schedule" and "Generate recommended schedules". This will prompt the user to enter hours per semester and then generate all semesters all the way up to graduation.

8. If the user decides they want to continue later, they may simply save their information under "File" and "Save current data".

9. If the user is stuck and needs some instruction, review these tutorials again or look under "Help" for more instructions as shown here.

10. Once the user has been satisfied with their schedule, they may go under "File" and "Print report" to generate a document to print and take to the advisor for their next degree planning appointment.
